Wedding Invitations

Two wedding invitation suites designed to match the personalities of the bride and groom.

Max and Rhiannon are an adorably nerdy couple. They wanted their wedding invitations to reflect their quirky personalities but also wanted a classic feel: a geeky Audrey Hepburn. Combining their favourite things (Firefly, The Legend of Zelda, The Twilight Zone, Ironman, Star Wars, Star Trek, Boarderlands, and Mass Effect) I designed a subtle invitation suite in the colours of Ironman.

The suite included the invitation, a directions card, and a response card that fit into a shimmering gold envelope, tied with a vibrant red bow.

My husband and I had an October wedding. We wanted a simple, clean look and feel to our invitations to reflect the Fall season. I created a pumpkin illustration to represent the two of us that we used throughout the invitation suite and our wedding. The cursive font I designed myself and used it to make the invitations have a more personal feel, rather than using a "standard" wedding font that anyone could use.

The full invitation suite included a save the date postcard, the invitation, a directions card, a response card, and a thank you postcard.

After we returned from our honeymoon in beautiful Hawaii we combined images from our wedding and the honeymoon to send Mahalo postcards to our guests. They were a big hit!

What I Learned

Designing invitation suits are so much fun. It really makes you consider design as a whole and how everything works together. Attention to detail is big because one little omition could mean the difference between people finding your wedding location easily or getting calls on your wedding day asking what town it is in.

Organization is key. Wedding invitations include many pieces: the invite, response card, directions card, etc. Design files must be organized and easy to access, while final printed copies must be all assembled and sorted into envelopes and addresses afixed. There is a reason so many companies offer to send your invitations for you so you don't have to lift a finger.

Personalized invitations really do set the tone of what is to come. Templates are easy but designing invitations to match the personalities of the couple and the wedding's theme really goes a long way to making such a large and important event special.
